## What is Virtual Reality (VR)?

Virtual reality (VR) is a technology that uses computer-generated environments to create a completely immersive experience for the user. In other words, it is like being transported to another world, where you can interact with your surroundings and live out experiences that may not be possible in real life. VR technology often involves using a headset with a screen or screens that are placed up close to the user's face, completely blocking out the real world and replacing it with a simulated environment.

## What is Augmented Reality (AR)?

Augmented reality (AR) is a technology that overlays digital information or virtual objects onto the real world, adding an extra layer of information to the user's environment. AR technology can be accessed through a wide variety of devices, including smartphones, tablets, and glasses. In AR, the user's real-world environment forms the backdrop for the digital information or objects, creating a blended experience that enhances the user's perception of reality. 

## Key Differences Between VR and AR

While both VR and AR provide immersive experiences for their users, there are some key differences between the two technologies that set them apart. One of the main differences is the level of immersion. With VR, the user is completely immersed in a simulated environment, with no outside distractions. With AR, the user is still connected to the real world, but with added digital information overlaid on it. 

Another key difference is the hardware used to access the technologies. In VR, the user must wear a headset with screens that are placed very close to the user's eyes, blocking out the real world. In AR, the user can access the technology through devices such as smartphones, tablets, or glasses, which do not fully block out the real world.

## Potential Applications for VR and AR

Healthcare is another sector where both VR and AR are making waves, with applications ranging from training medical professionals to treating mental health conditions.
