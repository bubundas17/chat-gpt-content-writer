Domain names are an integral part of the internet, but today's domain name system is a far cry from what it used to be. In this article, we'll take a look at the evolution of domain name registration since the early days of the internet.

In the early days of the internet, domain names were not as important as IP addresses. IP addresses are a series of numbers that identify a unique device on the internet. However, as the internet became more popular, it became apparent that IP addresses were difficult to remember. This is where domain names come in.

The very first domain name was registered in 1985. It was symbolics.com and was registered by the Symbolics Computer Corporation. From there, domain names exploded in popularity. In 1987, there were just a few hundred domain names in existence. By 1992, there were over 15,000.
 
It wasn't until 1998 that the Internet Corporation for Assigned Names and Numbers (ICANN) was created. ICANN is responsible for managing the domain name system and ensuring that domain names are unique and not duplicated. Since its creation, ICANN has played a critical role in the evolution of domain name registration.
 
One of the most significant changes to domain name registration was the introduction of top level domains (TLDs). TLDs are the last part of the domain name, such as .com, .org, or .net. The first TLDs were .com, .org, .net, .gov, .edu, and .mil. 
Today, there are over 1,500 top level domains, providing more options for domain name registration than ever before. 

Another important change was the creation of the domain name system (DNS). DNS is the system that translates domain names into IP addresses. Without DNS, we would have to remember IP addresses to access websites instead of easy-to-remember domain names.

As domain names became more popular, the process of registering a domain name became more expensive. Today, it can cost anywhere from a few dollars to tens of thousands of dollars to register a domain name, depending on the TLD.

In recent years, there have been discussions about the need for a new system to replace the current domain name system. Some have proposed blockchain technology as a replacement, while others have suggested a peer-to-peer system. Only time will tell what the future of domain name registration will look like.

In conclusion, the evolution of domain name registration has been a fascinating journey. From the earliest days of the internet to the present, domain names have played a critical role in making the internet more accessible and user-friendly.
