Both the Macbook Air and Dell XPS 13 offer sleek, premium designs that are sure to turn heads. The Macbook Air features Apple's iconic aluminum unibody design, while the XPS 13 features a machined aluminum design with a carbon fiber palm rest.

The Macbook Air is available in two sizes: 11-inch and 13-inch. Both sizes are thin and lightweight, with the 11-inch model weighing only 2.38 pounds and the 13-inch model weighing 2.96 pounds. The XPS 13, on the other hand, is only available in a 13-inch model, which weighs in at 2.7 pounds.

Overall, both ultrabooks offer impressive designs, but the Macbook Air's smaller size and lightweight design may give it an edge for those who prioritize portability.

### Display

The display is one of the most important aspects of any ultrabook, and both the Macbook Air and Dell XPS 13 deliver impressive results. 

The Macbook Air features a 13.3-inch LED-backlit display with a resolution of 1440 x 900 pixels. While this resolution may seem low compared to some other ultrabooks on the market, the quality of the display is impressive, with sharp text and vivid colors.

The XPS 13, on the other hand, features a 13.3-inch InfinityEdge display with a resolution of 1920 x 1080 pixels. This display offers a higher resolution and better color accuracy than the Macbook Air.

### Performance

Both the Macbook Air and Dell XPS 13 are powered by the latest Intel processors, but there are some differences in the hardware configurations and performance.

The XPS 13 is also available with either an Intel Core i5 or i7 processor, but has more configuration options, with up to 16GB of RAM and up to 2TB of SSD storage. 

In benchmark tests, the more powerful configurations of the XPS 13 outperform the Macbook Air, but the differences may not be noticeable for everyday users. Both ultrabooks offer more than enough performance for typical office tasks, web browsing, and streaming media.

### Battery Life

Battery life is another important consideration when choosing an ultrabook, and both the Macbook Air and Dell XPS 13 offer impressive results.

Overall, both ultrabooks offer impressive battery life, with the Macbook Air offering slightly better results for video playback and the Dell XPS 13 offering slightly better results for web browsing.

### Price

Now comes the ultimate question: which ultrabook offers better value for the money?

The Dell XPS 13 starts at $999 for the base model, with an Intel Core i3 processor, 4GB of RAM, and 128GB of SSD storage. Higher-end configurations can cost over $2,000.
