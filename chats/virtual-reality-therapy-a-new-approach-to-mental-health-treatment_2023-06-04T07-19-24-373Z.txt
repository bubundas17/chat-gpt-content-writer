Virtual reality therapy is particularly useful in exposing individuals to situations they fear the most. For instance, someone who suffers from a fear of flying can use VRT to simulate a plane ride, and gradually expose themselves to the perceived threat in a controlled environment.

Virtual reality therapy is not just limited to addressing mental health concerns. It's also used to improve various cognitive abilities like memory, attention, and focus. VRT is a powerful tool in addressing these mental traits, and it's currently used to improve cognitive functions in individuals with neurological disorders, intellectual disabilities, traumatic brain injuries, and more.
