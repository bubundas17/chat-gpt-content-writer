Are you looking to improve your mobile website’s search engine ranking? Look no further than AMP, or Accelerated Mobile Pages. As the name suggests, AMP is a tool designed to speed up the loading time of mobile websites. But did you know that using AMP can also have a huge impact on your website’s SEO? 

In this article, we’ll go over the basics of AMP and how it can boost your mobile website’s search engine ranking. We’ll also share tips on how to implement AMP correctly and how to measure its impact on your website’s SEO.

What Are Accelerated Mobile Pages (AMP)?

Accelerated Mobile Pages (AMP) are essentially lightweight versions of web pages that are designed to load quickly on mobile devices. They are built using a stripped-down version of HTML, JavaScript, and CSS, and are hosted on Google AMP Cache, which uses Google’s own servers to deliver these pages faster to mobile users.

AMP pages are designed to be mobile-friendly, image-heavy, and aesthetically pleasing. They load lightning fast, typically in less than a second, and provide a seamless, user-friendly experience for mobile users.

Why Is AMP Important for SEO?

The main reason why AMP is important for SEO is that Google favors websites that load quickly on mobile devices. In fact, website speed is now a ranking factor for Google, as slow-loading websites can result in a higher bounce rate and lower user engagement.

By implementing AMP on your mobile website, you can improve its loading speed, reduce page bounce rates, and increase user engagement. This can lead to higher search engine rankings, more traffic, and ultimately, more conversions and revenue for your business.

Moreover, Google has explicitly stated that AMP pages will appear in their own carousel at the top of mobile search results, giving them more visibility and a higher click-through rate. This means that even if your website isn't the top-ranked on Google, AMP can still give you a chance to occupy the coveted top spot on mobile search results.

How to Implement AMP Correctly

Implementing AMP on your mobile website is relatively straightforward, but it does require a few steps:

Step 1: Install the AMP Plugin

If you have a WordPress website, you can simply install the AMP plugin to create AMP versions of your pages. The plugin will create a separate AMP version of each of your web pages and add a special tag to your original web pages that tells Google to index the AMP versions.

Step 2: Validate Your AMP Pages

To ensure that your AMP pages are valid and compliant with Google’s guidelines, you’ll need to use the AMP Validation Tool. This tool will check your pages for errors and provide suggestions on how to fix them.

Step 3: Submit Your AMP Pages to Google

After you’ve created and validated your AMP pages, you’ll need to submit them to Google using Google Search Console. This way, Google can recognize and index your AMP pages for mobile search results.

Measuring the Impact of AMP on Your SEO

The best way to measure the impact of AMP on your SEO is to track your website’s performance using Google Analytics. By comparing the performance of your regular mobile pages with your AMP pages, you can gauge the difference in loading time, bounce rates, and user engagement.

If your AMP pages are performing better than your regular mobile pages, it’s a good sign that your website’s SEO is improving. However, if they’re not performing as well as expected, you’ll need to make adjustments and follow best practices to ensure that your mobile website is optimized for AMP.

Conclusion

AMP is a powerful tool that can have a significant impact on your mobile website’s SEO. By improving your website’s loading speed and user engagement, you can increase your chances of ranking higher on search engine results pages and driving more traffic to your website.

Implementing AMP on your mobile website is relatively easy, but it does require following best practices and proper validation. By using Google Analytics to track your website’s performance, you can measure the impact of AMP on your SEO and continually improve your website’s mobile optimization for maximum results.
