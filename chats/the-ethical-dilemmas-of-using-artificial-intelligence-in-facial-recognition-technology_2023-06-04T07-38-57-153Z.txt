However, with the involvement of artificial intelligence (AI), facial recognition technology has undergone a revolution. AI algorithms have the capability to detect patterns that the human brain does not recognize quickly.

###Unreliability

One critical ethical implication of AI in facial recognition technology is unreliability. Many studies have found that facial recognition technology is less reliable when it comes to identifying people of color and women. Moreover, AI algorithms can be biased, leading to inaccurate facial recognition results. These inaccuracies can lead to wrongful accusations and even arrest for innocent individuals, resulting in severe consequences that can follow them for the rest of their lives.

###Privacy Concerns

Another ethical issue with AI facial recognition technology is privacy concerns. In this modern world, the use of cameras, smartphones and drones often leads to people feeling uncomfortable and violating their privacy. Such technology can quickly identify people within a locality without their knowledge. The increasing adoption of facial recognition technology in public spaces can violate the privacy of individuals, making them feel monitored at all times.

###Surveillance

The use of AI facial recognition technology also raises concerns about surveillance culture. When used in public spaces, this technology makes it easier for authorities to monitor people's movements, even if they are not in any way breaking the law. The government can use the technology to monitor the locations, activities and movements of citizens, which many people argue is a violation of their fundamental human rights.
