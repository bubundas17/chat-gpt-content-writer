Email Forwarding: How it Works and How to Use it

Email forwarding is a powerful tool that can help you manage your email more efficiently and stay organized. It allows you to automatically send emails from one address to another, so you can access all your emails in one place. In this guide, we’ll take a closer look at how email forwarding works and how you can use it to streamline your inbox.

## What is Email Forwarding and How Does it Work?

Email forwarding is a feature that allows you to automatically forward emails sent to one email address to another email address. This can be useful for a number of reasons, including:

- Consolidating multiple email accounts: If you have multiple email accounts, you can set up email forwarding to consolidate all your emails in one place.
- Collaborating with others: If you’re working on a project with other people, you can set up email forwarding to automatically forward emails to everyone involved.
- Staying organized: You can use email forwarding to automatically sort your emails into separate folders based on the sender, subject, or other criteria.

To set up email forwarding, you’ll need to have access to the email account you want to forward from and the email account you want to forward to. Most email services provide an option for setting up email forwarding in their settings menu. Here’s how to set up email forwarding in Gmail, for example:

1. Open Gmail and log in to the account you want to forward emails from.
2. Click the gear icon in the top right corner of the screen and select “Settings” from the drop-down menu.
3. Click the “Forwarding and POP/IMAP” tab.
4. Click the “Add a forwarding address” button and enter the email address you want to forward to.
5. Follow the instructions to verify the forwarding address.
6. Choose what you want to happen to your Gmail copy of the email. You can keep it in your inbox, mark it as read, archive it, or delete it.
7. Click “Save Changes” to enable email forwarding.

Once you’ve set up email forwarding, any emails that are sent to your original email address will be automatically forwarded to the email address you specified.

## How to Use Email Forwarding to Streamline Your Inbox

Now that you know how email forwarding works, let’s take a look at some of the ways you can use it to streamline your inbox.

### Consolidate Multiple Email Accounts

If you have multiple email accounts, you can use email forwarding to consolidate all your emails in one place. For example, if you have a personal email account, a work email account, and a school email account, you can set up email forwarding to forward all your emails to your personal email account. This way, you only have to check one inbox to see all your emails.

### Automatically Sort Your Emails

You can also use email forwarding to automatically sort your emails into separate folders based on the sender, subject, or other criteria. For example, you can set up email forwarding to automatically forward all emails from your boss to a “Work” folder, or all emails with the word “invoice” in the subject line to a “Finances” folder. This can make it easier to keep track of your emails and find what you need quickly.

### Collaborate with Others

If you’re working on a project with other people, you can use email forwarding to automatically forward emails to everyone involved. For example, if you’re working on a group project for school, you can set up email forwarding to automatically forward all emails related to the project to everyone in the group. This can help ensure that everyone is on the same page and has access to the same information.

### Keep Your Email Organized

Email forwarding can also help you keep your email organized by removing clutter from your inbox. For example, if you subscribe to a lot of newsletters or mailing lists, you can set up email forwarding to automatically forward these emails to a “Subscription” folder. This way, your inbox will only contain emails that require your immediate attention.
