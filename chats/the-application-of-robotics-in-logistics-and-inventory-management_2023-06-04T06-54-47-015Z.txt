Automated Guided Vehicles (AGVs) are robots that can transport loads within a warehouse. These robots can navigate through narrow aisles and tight spaces, providing an efficient way to move goods. The robots are controlled by a central computer system and can be programmed to transport materials, pallets, or other loads. With the use of sensors and cameras, these robots can detect obstacles and avoid collisions, making them safer than conventional forklifts. AGVs can operate around the clock, increasing warehouse efficiency and reducing labor costs. 

Robotic pick-and-place systems are another application of robotics technology in logistics. These robots can pick and place products from one location to another quickly and efficiently. They use sensors and cameras to detect the location and orientation of the items, ensuring accurate and precise movement. With their speed and precision, robotic pick-and-place systems can handle higher volumes of materials, reducing errors and increasing productivity. 

Robotic palletizers are machines that automate the process of building pallet loads. These robots can stack boxes or bags of products on pallets, optimizing space utilization and reducing material handling costs. The robots use sensors and cameras to detect the shape and size of the products, ensuring an accurate and efficient stacking process. Robotic palletizers can handle heavy loads and operate continuously, improving supply-chain efficiency. 

In addition to these applications, robotics technology is being used in inventory management to improve accuracy and efficiency. Robots can be used to perform inventory counts, reducing the need for physical inventory checks and saving time. With the use of sensors and cameras, robots can scan barcodes and read tags, making accurate counts and providing real-time inventory information. This information can be used to improve demand planning and reduce stock shortages.
