With nanobots, however, doctors can administer drugs to specific cells or organs, allowing for targeted treatment without affecting healthy tissue.

Nanobots can be engineered to deliver drugs more efficiently than traditional methods. For example, they can be designed to transport drugs across cell membranes and to penetrate the blood-brain barrier to treat brain tumors or neurological conditions. They can also be programmed to release the drug at a specific site or time, ensuring better efficacy and reduced side effects.

Surgery and Wound Healing Nanobots:
Nanobots can assist in surgeries, particularly in delicate operations where even the slightest error can be life-threatening.
