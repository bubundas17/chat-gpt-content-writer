TITLE: 15 Best Mobile Apps for Productivity in 2021 
META_DESCRIPTION: Discover the top mobile apps that can help you be more productive and efficient in your daily tasks. From note-taking to project management, find out which apps are worth downloading on your phone.
TAGS: productivity, mobile apps, organization, time management, task management, note-taking, project management, goal tracking.
CATEGORIES: Tutorials, How-to Guides, Product Comparisons, Tips and Tricks.

If you're someone who is constantly striving to achieve more in less time, then having the right mobile apps on your phone can make a big difference. With the numerous applications available today, it can be challenging to sift through them all and determine which ones are worth downloading to help you boost your productivity. That's why we've put together a list of the best mobile apps for productivity in 2021.

1. Todoist

Todoist is a task management app that allows you to create lists, projects, and subtasks for all your daily to-dos. You can set priority levels, deadlines, and recurring tasks to help you stay organized and on top of your work. Todoist syncs across all devices, making it easy to access your tasks wherever you are.

2. Evernote

Evernote is a note-taking app that lets you take, organize, and share notes, documents, and photos across multiple devices. You can also use Evernote to create to-do lists, set reminders, and collaborate with team members or friends.

3. Trello

Trello is a project management app that uses boards, cards, and lists to help you stay on top of your projects and tasks. You can share boards with team members and set due dates and notifications for all your tasks and projects.

4. Google Drive

Google Drive is a cloud-based storage solution that allows you to store, access, and share files across multiple devices. You can also use Google Docs and Sheets to collaborate on documents with team members in real-time.

5. Forest

Forest is a unique productivity app that helps you stay focused and avoid distractions. The app rewards you with a virtual tree every time you stay focused for a predetermined amount of time. Over time, you can grow a virtual forest and build a habit of being productive.

6. Focus@Will

If you're someone who works better with background noise, Focus@Will is the app for you. The app streams music specifically designed to increase focus and productivity. You can choose from different genres and styles to personalize your experience.

7. Noisli

Noisli is another app that plays ambient background noise to help you focus and be productive. You can choose from a variety of sounds, including rainfall, waves, and birds chirping.

8. RescueTime

RescueTime is a time management app that tracks how you spend your time on your phone. The app gives you reports and insights on how you use your phone and provides suggestions on how to be more productive.

9. Pocket

Pocket is an app that lets you save articles, videos, and other content to read later. You can save content from your phone, tablet, or computer and read it offline later. Pocket also allows you to tag content and organize it for easy access later.

10. Focus@Work

Focus@Work is a time management app that uses the Pomodoro technique to help you stay focused and productive. You work in 25-minute intervals with 5-minute breaks in between. After four sessions, you take a longer break.

11. Asana

Asana is a project management app that allows you to create projects and assign tasks to team members. You can set deadlines, add comments and attachments, and even view progress charts to see how much work has been completed.

12. Slack

Slack is a messaging app specifically designed for teams. You can create channels for different projects, discuss issues, and even share files directly in the app. Slack also integrates with other apps like Google Drive and Trello.

13. Microsoft To Do

Microsoft To Do is a task management app that allows you to create tasks and subtasks. You can set due dates, reminders, and even attach notes and files to your tasks. Microsoft To Do syncs across all devices, making it easy to access your tasks from anywhere.

14. Notion

Notion is an all-in-one workspace that allows you to create notes, tasks, wikis, and databases all in one place. You can even create templates for recurring tasks and projects. Notion also syncs across all devices, making it easy to access your work from anywhere.

15. Habitica

Habitica is a unique productivity app that gamifies your daily tasks and habits. You create an avatar and gain experience and rewards for completing tasks and building habits. You can also join guilds and compete with other Habitica users.

In conclusion, these mobile apps can help you manage your time, stay organized, and be more productive overall. Try out some of the above apps and see which ones work best for you. Good luck, and happy app-ing!
