With the vast array of productivity apps and tools available on the market, it can be overwhelming to choose which one to try. 1. RescueTime

RescueTime is an app that tracks how much time you spend on various websites and applications throughout the day. The app then provides you with a report on how much time you spend on productive tasks and how much time you waste on distractions. You can also set goals for yourself and receive reports on your progress. It is an excellent tool for those who need to manage their time more effectively.

2. Trello

Trello is a project management app that uses a board and card system to help you organize your tasks. It allows you to create boards for different projects and add cards with tasks that need to be accomplished. You can also assign tasks to team members and track the progress of each task. Trello is an excellent app for teams who need to collaborate efficiently.

3. Todoist

Todoist is a task management app that allows you to create tasks and organize them into projects and categories. You can set due dates, reminders, and priorities for each task. The app also has a feature called "Karma," which rewards you with points when you complete tasks on time. Todoist is perfect for those who need to manage multiple tasks in various categories.

4. Focus@Will

Focus@Will is an app that provides you with music designed to help you focus. The music is specially curated to increase your productivity and concentration. You can choose from several genres, including classical, jazz, and ambient. Focus@Will is perfect for those who need help concentrating at work or while studying.

5. Pocket

Pocket is an app that allows you to save articles, videos, and other content to read later. It integrates with your browser and allows you to save content with one click. You can then access the content from any device, even without an internet connection. Pocket is perfect for those who want to save time by bookmarking content for later reading.

6. Otter Voice Notes

Otter Voice Notes is an app that transcribes your voice into text. It is perfect for those who need to take notes quickly but are not good at typing. You can use it to transcribe meetings, discussions, or even lectures. Otter Voice Notes is an excellent tool for those who need to keep track of information on the go.
