The Evolution of Travel Industry

The travel industry has seen significant changes with the progress of technology. Each year, it seems that the industry adopts new technologies to make travel simpler, convenient and enjoyable for tourists. Innovations like online ticket-booking, self-check-ins, electronic boarding passes, and digital room keys have made the travel experience much smoother.

Despite such advancements, tourists still face issues like language barriers and finding their way around an unknown place. This is where AR comes in, providing travelers with new innovative ways of exploring new destinations.

Applications of Augmented Reality in the Travel Industry

AR technology has expanded the possibilities of how travelers can explore new destinations. Here are some of the ways AR is transforming the travel industry:

Virtual Tours:  AR technology can bring destinations to life with virtual tours. Tourists can experience historical sites or remote locations, while feeling like they are physically present at the location.

Virtual Maps: AR-powered maps can help tourists navigate their way around an area. By embedding augmented reality technology in traditional maps, tourists can scan street signs, blurbs and photos of a place. Edifices and landmarks can be easily identified by simply holding a smartphone up.

Tourism Guides: AR can provide visual help to tourists with information on local landmarks, cultural events and more. Smartphones and tablets can identify locations that tourists are passing by and provide real-time information to assist them in understanding local customs, historical facts and much more.

Other applications of AR in the travel industry include multi-lingual translation technology, immersive experiences, and enabling travellers to experience attractions in an entirely new way.

Benefits of AR in the Travel Industry

AR technology is more than just another tool for information. It provides an immersive experience that can leave tourists awestruck and connected with their surroundings.
