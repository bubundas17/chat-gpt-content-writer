Overview of the Health Care Reform Bill

The American Health Care Act is a proposed bill that aims to repeal and replace the Affordable Care Act, also known as Obamacare. The bill was introduced in March 2017 by the House of Representatives and passed in May the same year. However, it has not been enacted as it has failed to pass the Senate on multiple occasions.

The bill proposes significant changes to the healthcare system, including the elimination of the individual mandate, which requires everyone to buy health insurance or pay a penalty. It also seeks to expand the use of Health Saving Accounts (HSAs) and provides tax credits to individuals to help them purchase insurance.

The health care reform bill has been met with significant criticism from various players in the healthcare industry as well as politicians. Another significant criticism of the bill is that it seeks to cut Medicaid funding significantly. Medicaid is a program that provides health insurance to low-income individuals and families.
