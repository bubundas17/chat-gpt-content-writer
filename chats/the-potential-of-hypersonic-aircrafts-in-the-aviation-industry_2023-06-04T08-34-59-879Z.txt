1. Reduced Travel Times
Hypersonic aircrafts could significantly reduce travel times for passengers. For example, a flight from New York to London that currently takes seven hours could be completed in just two hours with a hypersonic aircraft.

2. Increased Efficiency
Hypersonic aircrafts are expected to be more fuel-efficient than traditional aircrafts. This is because they travel at higher altitudes, where the air is less dense and there is less air resistance.

3. Military Applications
Hypersonic aircrafts also have tremendous military potential. They can be used for reconnaissance, surveillance, and intelligence gathering. They can also be equipped with weapons and used for strategic strikes.

However, like any new technology, hypersonic aircrafts come with their own set of challenges. Here are some of the main challenges that need to be addressed before hypersonic aircrafts can become a reality:

2. Safety
Hypersonic flight is also more dangerous than traditional flight. The extreme speeds and temperatures can cause damage to the aircraft and put passengers at risk.

3. Noise Pollution
Hypersonic aircrafts generate a lot of noise during takeoff and landing. This can be problematic for residents living near airports.
