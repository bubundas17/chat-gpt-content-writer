Cloud gaming will require faster internet speeds and lower latency to provide a seamless gaming experience. This means that internet service providers (ISPs) will need to provide faster internet speeds and upgrade their networks to support the increased demand for online gaming.

This means that ISPs will need to increase their data allotments to accommodate the increased demand for data-intensive activities like cloud gaming.

Another impact of cloud gaming on internet infrastructure is the need for low latency. Latency is the amount of time it takes for data to travel from the device to the cloud server and back. In online gaming, low latency is crucial as it affects the gameplay experience. High latency can lead to lag and affect the game’s responsiveness, making it unplayable. ISP’s will need to invest in latency-reducing technologies to support the increasing demand for low-latency cloud gaming.
