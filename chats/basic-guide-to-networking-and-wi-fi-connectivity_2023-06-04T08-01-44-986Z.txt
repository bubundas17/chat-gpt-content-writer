## What is Networking?
Networking is the process of connecting two or more devices together to enable communication and resource sharing. Devices may include computers, printers, scanners, servers, and more. Networks can be categorized into two types: **Wired Networks** and **Wireless Networks**.

### Wired Networks
Wired networks use physical cables to connect devices together. Ethernet cables are commonly used for this purpose. When setting up a wired network, you need to ensure that all devices are connected to a switch or hub. This device will act as a central point for all communication.

### Wireless Networks
Wireless networks use radio waves to connect devices together. To set up a wireless network, you need a router that will act as a central point for all communication. Devices can connect to the router using Wi-Fi.

## Setting Up Your Router
To set up your router, follow these steps:

1. Connect your router to your modem using an Ethernet cable.
2. Connect your computer to the router using an Ethernet cable or Wi-Fi.
3. Access the router's web-based setup page. You can find the IP address of your router on the bottom of the device.
4. Follow the router's setup wizard. You will need to create a username and password.

## Securing Your Network
It's essential to secure your network to prevent unauthorized access. Here are a few steps you can take:

1. Change the default username and password of your router.
2. Enable WPA2 encryption on your router to secure your Wi-Fi network.
3. Disable remote access to your router.
4. Regularly update your router's firmware.

## Troubleshooting Connectivity Issues
If you're experiencing connectivity issues, try the following:

1. Restart your router and modem.
2. Check your Wi-Fi password.
3. Ensure that your devices are within range of the router.
4. Disable any firewalls on your computer.
5. Check for any firmware updates for your router.
