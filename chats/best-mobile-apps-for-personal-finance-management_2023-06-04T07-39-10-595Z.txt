1. Mint: Mint is one of the most popular personal finance management apps out there. It lets you view all your accounts in one place, create a budget, track your spending, and get personalized financial advice.

2. PocketGuard: This app helps you create a budget and shows you how much money you have available to spend. It also provides insight into your spending habits and gives you personalized tips on how to save more money.

3. Acorns: Acorns is a unique app that allows you to invest with spare change from your purchases. It links to your bank account and rounds up your purchases to the nearest dollar, then invests the difference in diversified portfolios.

4. You Need a Budget (YNAB): YNAB is a budgeting app that helps you stay on top of your finances by giving you full control over your money. It forces you to plan your spending and save for big-ticket items, helping you avoid debt and achieve your financial goals.

5. Personal Capital: Personal Capital is a personal finance management app that lets you view all your accounts in one place. It also offers detailed investment tracking, retirement planning tools, and personalized financial advice.

6. Goodbudget: Goodbudget is a budgeting app that uses the envelope budgeting technique to help you stay on track. It lets you set up virtual envelopes for different spending categories and allocate funds accordingly.

7. Honeydue: This app is designed specifically for couples who want to manage their finances together. It lets you set budgets, track your spending, and even pay bills together.

8. Expensify: Expensify is a great app for anyone who needs to manage business expenses. It lets you track receipts, create expense reports, and even submit receipts for reimbursement.

9. Clarity Money: Clarity Money is a personal finance management app that offers a range of features, including budgeting tools, account tracking, and even subscription management.

10. Wally: Wally is a budgeting app that lets you track your spending, set financial goals, and even get alerts when bills are due. It's a great app for anyone looking to take control of their finances.

In conclusion, there are plenty of apps out there that can help you manage your personal finances.
