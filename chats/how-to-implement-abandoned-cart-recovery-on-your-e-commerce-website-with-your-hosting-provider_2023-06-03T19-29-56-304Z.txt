Abandoned cart is a common issue in e-commerce sites. Shopping cart abandonment happens when a customer adds items to their cart but leaves the website without completing the purchase. This can happen for various reasons like unexpected costs, lack of trust, website errors, or simply getting distracted. To reduce the impact of abandoned carts on online sales, e-commerce businesses use abandoned cart recovery strategies.

One of the most popular and effective abandoned cart recovery strategies is email reminders. By sending targeted reminder emails to customers, businesses can recover lost revenue from abandoned carts. However, implementing abandoned cart recovery on your e-commerce website can be tricky, especially if you don't have technical expertise. Fortunately, some hosting providers offer integrated abandoned cart recovery solutions.

After enabling the feature, you need to configure the abandoned cart recovery settings. Typically, you'll need to set up email templates, frequency, and triggers. Creating effective reminder emails is vital to the success of your abandoned cart recovery strategy. There are a few best practices to follow when creating reminder emails:
