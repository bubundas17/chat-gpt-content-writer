Internet surveillance refers to the monitoring of online activities, such as browsing history and emails, with the aim of gathering data for various purposes. Governments and corporations use internet surveillance to improve cybersecurity, detect criminal and terrorist activities, and target advertising campaigns.

However, mass surveillance practices have raised serious concerns about personal privacy and digital connectivity. Governments can intercept online communications, collect personal data without consent, monitor internet traffic, and track individuals' online behavior without transparency or accountability.

The impact of internet surveillance on personal privacy is significant. Individuals have a right to privacy, and online surveillance can be used to violate this right. Mass surveillance undermines the trust between individuals and the state, stifling free speech, and inhibiting the free exchange of ideas. Moreover, it can lead to self-censorship and curtail innovation.

Furthermore, internet surveillance can compromise cybersecurity. If surveillance data is mishandled, it can be stolen by hackers or other unauthorized parties. Also, individuals may be more vulnerable to phishing attacks and other cyber threats if their personal data is compromised.

To mitigate the impact of internet surveillance on personal privacy and digital connectivity, governments and corporations must take measures to promote transparency and accountability. Also, individuals must take steps to protect their online privacy, such as using encrypted communication services, using strong passwords, and regularly updating their systems' security.

In conclusion, internet surveillance has become a contentious issue affecting personal privacy and digital connectivity.
