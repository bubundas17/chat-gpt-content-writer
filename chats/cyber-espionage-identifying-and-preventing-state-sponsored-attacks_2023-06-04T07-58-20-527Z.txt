The digital era has brought about a lot of conveniences for individuals and corporations alike. However, it has also brought an equal share of bad actors that relentlessly exploit cyber vulnerabilities for illicit purposes. Chief among these bad actors are state-sponsored groups that use cutting-edge cyber espionage tactics to infiltrate and steal from target organizations. 

As per the Council on Foreign Relations, state-sponsored cyber attacks trace their history back to the 1990s. Nowadays, foreign countries use cyber-espionage tactics to gain, exploit, and maintain a strategic advantage over their adversaries. Espionage activities are rooted in geopolitical and military interests and can take different forms, including data exfiltration, sabotage, and the spread of misinformation. 

State-sponsored cyber attacks can cause significant damage to individuals, corporations, and countries. In 2014, the Sony Pictures cyber attack, which was attributed to North Korea, brought to the forefront nation-state actors' power in wreaking havoc on foreign entities through cyber espionage. It's no longer enough for corporations to focus mainly on data breaches and other traditional cyber threats; they must also be vigilant against state-sponsored cyber attacks.

Here are some steps corporations can take to identifying and prevent state-sponsored attacks:

## Understanding the threat landscape

The first step in identifying and preventing state-sponsored attacks is to conduct a thorough risk assessment of your organization. This assessment should seek to understand what information is valuable to state actors, identify worst-case scenarios, and strive to resolve system vulnerabilities that can allow attackers access to sensitive information. Organizations must keep tabs on the latest tactics, techniques, and procedures nation-state actors use to identify any irregularities and patch them up fast.

## Securing user accounts

Nation-state actors aim to breach organizations through their users, and this means their focus will be on gaining unauthorized access to user accounts. Hence, it is essential to adopt defensive measures like two-factor authentication and impose a strict password policy that involves altering passwords regularly. Promoting cybersecurity awareness training within your organization can also help eliminate risks from the human element to get your users more involved in spotting phishing attempts.

## Implementing endpoint security measures

Endpoint security measures are critical in preventing all forms of cyber threats, including things like ransomware. Corporations should implement endpoint security measures such as advanced threat protection, web security filtering, and attachment scanning to safeguard against a range of threats.

## Monitoring network traffic

Corporations can employ network traffic monitoring systems capable of detecting any anomalies or suspicious activity on their networks, which helps in identifying possible breaches in the system. Monitoring network traffic can also help in recognizing threats other than state-sponsored attacks.

## Continuous maintenance of security infrastructure

Preventing state-sponsored attacks is not a one-time affair. It requires continued maintenance and updating of the organization's security infrastructure to detect and handle emerging threats. Therefore, regular system penetration testing and security audits are essential to stay on top of threats and vulnerabilities.

In conclusion, the threat of cyber espionage from nation-state actors is here to stay. Corporations must be vigilant against these types of threats by understanding the threat landscape, securing user accounts, implementing endpoint security measures, monitoring network traffic and maintaining an up-to-date security infrastructure. By doing so, your organization can proactively identify and prevent attacks from nation-state actors.
