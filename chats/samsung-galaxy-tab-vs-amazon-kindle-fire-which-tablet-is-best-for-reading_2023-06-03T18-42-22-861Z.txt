**Design and Display**

The Samsung Galaxy Tab has a 10.1-inch screen with a resolution of 1920 x 1200 pixels. It is powered by an octa-core processor and comes with 2GB of RAM and 32GB of internal storage. The tablet has a sleek and slim design that makes it comfortable to hold and easy to carry around.

The Amazon Kindle Fire, on the other hand, has a 7-inch screen with a resolution of 1024 x 600 pixels. It is powered by a quad-core processor and comes with 1GB of RAM and 16GB of internal storage. The tablet has a simple design with a plastic back that makes it easy to grip.

When it comes to reading, the Samsung Galaxy Tab's larger and higher resolution screen offers a better reading experience. The text is clearer and crisper, and it is easier to read larger documents and books without having to constantly zoom in and out.

**Software and Apps**

Both the Galaxy Tab and the Kindle Fire run on modified versions of Android. The Galaxy Tab features Samsung's TouchWiz UI, while the Kindle Fire uses Amazon's Fire OS. While both UIs offer a smooth and fluid experience, TouchWiz is more customizable and allows for more flexibility in terms of customizing the device.

As for apps, the Kindle Fire comes pre-loaded with the Amazon Appstore, which offers a wide selection of apps, books, magazines, and content. The Galaxy Tab, on the other hand, comes with Google Play Store, which offers an even wider range of apps and content.

**Battery Life**

In terms of battery life, both the Galaxy Tab and the Kindle Fire offer reasonable battery life. The Galaxy Tab can last up to 13 hours on a single charge, while the Kindle Fire can last up to 8 hours.

**Price**

Price is an important consideration for many people when it comes to purchasing a tablet. The Samsung Galaxy Tab is significantly more expensive than the Amazon Kindle Fire, with a starting price of $329. The Kindle Fire, on the other hand, starts at just $49.99.

**Conclusion**

When it comes to reading, the Samsung Galaxy Tab offers a better reading experience thanks to its larger and higher resolution display. If you are on a budget, the Kindle Fire is a great option that offers a decent reading experience at a fraction of the cost of the Galaxy Tab. However, if you want the best possible reading experience and are willing to pay for it, the Galaxy Tab is the better choice.
