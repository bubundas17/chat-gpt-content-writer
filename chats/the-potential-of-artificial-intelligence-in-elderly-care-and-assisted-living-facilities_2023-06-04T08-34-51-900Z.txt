Improving Quality of Care
AI has the potential to improve the quality of care provided to elderly patients by facilitating personalized care plans based on their needs and preferences. With AI-based medical algorithms, it is possible to identify patterns, predict potential health issues and provide proactive care to prevent or manage incidents. These features can help doctors and caregivers to support patients living with dementia, Alzheimer’s and other age-related chronic health conditions.

Fall detection is one of the most important features of AI for elderly people living alone or in a group. With falls being the leading cause of injury and death amongst the elderly, AI-powered fall detection systems can help identify when a fall has occurred, and alert caregivers to provide immediate assistance. Additionally, AI-powered sensors can monitor patient’s movement and activities to ensure that they are eating, taking medication and exercising regularly.

Reducing Costs
Adopting AI-based healthcare systems can help reduce costs for patients, healthcare providers and facilities. As AI can perform complex and repetitive tasks, it can substantially lower the burden on healthcare professionals, freeing up their time to focus on more complex tasks. AI-powered virtual assistants and chatbots, for instance, can answer patients' mundane queries, allowing doctors to focus their attention on those patients who need care.

Moreover, AI-based systems can enhance patient’s safety while avoiding costly accidents and incidents. Smart sensors, motion detectors, and facial recognition technology are powerful tools that can identify potential risks and act proactively. All these benefits indirectly translate to cost savings for elderly care facilities and reduce the cost of healthcare insurance premiums for patients.
