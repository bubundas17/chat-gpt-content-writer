One of the most promising areas of biofuel research is the use of algae. They are also able to grow in a variety of conditions, including wastewater and even saltwater. Algae have the potential to be a highly efficient source of biofuel, as they can produce high yields of oil, which can then be refined into biodiesel or other fuels.

Advances in genetic engineering are also being used to create strains of algae that are more productive and better suited to specific growing conditions.

Researchers are also exploring ways to use waste products, such as agricultural waste or municipal solid waste, as raw materials for biofuel production.
