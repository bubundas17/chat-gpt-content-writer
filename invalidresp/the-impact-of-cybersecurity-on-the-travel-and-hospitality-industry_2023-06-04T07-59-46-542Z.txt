CATEGORIES: Industry Analysis, Cybersecurity

The travel and hospitality industry has rapidly evolved, with technology driving a massive change in the industry. However, with all the benefits technology brings, it also increases the vulnerability of customer data to cybercriminals. From flight booking to hotel check-ins, travelers share personal information with a range of players in the travel industry. This presents a cyber attack surface that is too tempting for cybercriminals to ignore.

In this post, we’ll explore the importance of cybersecurity in the travel and hospitality industry. We will also discuss the potential risks travelers face when data is compromised, and how travelers can safeguard their personal information.

Why cybersecurity is crucial in the travel and hospitality industry
The travel industry is a massive target for cybercriminals. With millions of online bookings each day, cyber attacks can cause significant damage to both the business and the customer. Given the impact on revenue through reputational damage due to data breaches, the need to prioritize cybersecurity in the travel industry can’t be overstated. Here are a few ways cyber attacks can negatively affect the industry.

Identity theft
Cybercriminals have been known to target hotels' computer systems, stealing customers' personal and credit card information. In some cases, this information is used to commit identity theft, leaving the victim with a significant financial burden.

Phishing scams
Phishing scams, where customers are tricked into unwittingly sharing personal information, have become a huge problem in the travel industry. Cybercriminals have copied legitimate booking sites and made them look like the real thing to dupe customers and steal sensitive information.

Ransomware attacks
A ransomware attack is a type of malware that will hold data ransom, usually in exchange for money. A cybercriminal will encrypt the target's data, rendering it unreadable and unusable until payment is made.

How travelers can protect their personal information
As a traveler, you have a role to play in keeping your personal information safe. While you may not be able to control the security policies of airlines, hotel chains, or car rental companies, you can still take steps to secure your personal data.

Use strong passwords
The importance of a strong password cannot be overstated. A good password comprises multiple characters, numbers, and symbols, making it difficult to guess.

Be wary of public Wi-Fi
Public Wi-Fi is never one hundred percent secure and poses many cybersecurity risks. Never conduct financial transactions or access sensitive information while on public Wi-Fi, unless you have a VPN.

Use a VPN
A virtual private network (VPN) creates an encrypted connection between your device and the internet. This way, sensitive information is shielded from prying eyes.

Conclusion
With cybercrime on the rise, travelers must be vigilant in securing their personal information. The travel and hospitality industry has a significant role to play in ensuring that customers’ data is adequately secured. With the diverse range of cyber threats targeting the travel and hospitality sector, cybersecurity measures must be put in place to protect the industry and its customers from data breaches and other cyber threats.

By adopting best practices such as using strong passwords, avoiding public Wi-Fi, and using a VPN, travelers can proactively protect themselves against cyber threats. Meanwhile, companies must safeguard their customers' data by implementing robust cybersecurity measures, such as network security, encryption, and continuous monitoring, to mitigate the risks of data breaches.

In conclusion, ensuring cybersecurity in the travel and hospitality industry is vital, given the size and scope of the industry and how data-driven it has become. By staying informed and taking proactive measures, travelers can protect their personal information, and the industry can maintain the trust of its customers.
