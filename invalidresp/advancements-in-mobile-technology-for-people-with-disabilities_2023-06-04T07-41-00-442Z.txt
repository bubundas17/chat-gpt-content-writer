Mobile technology is also helping people with visual and hearing impairments. For example, screen-reading software can now read out loud text on a screen, and Braille displays can provide tactile feedback to those who are visually impaired. Smartphones and tablets can also be customized with larger fonts, high contrast displays, and special screen color filters to make them easier to use.

Similarly, hearing aids that are connected to mobile devices allow people with hearing impairments to stream calls and music directly to their ears. This can be especially helpful in noisy or crowded environments.

4. Augmented Reality
Augmented reality is another technology that has the potential to change the lives of people with disabilities.
