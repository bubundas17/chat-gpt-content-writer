When it comes to buying the perfect TV for a 4K viewing experience, there's a lot of factors to consider. Two of the most popular TV technologies in the market today are QLED and OLED TVs. Each of these technologies have their own distinct advantages and disadvantages. In this article, we'll compare the QLED and OLED TVs to help you make the right decision.

### What are QLED and OLED TVs?

First, let's understand what each of these technologies mean.

**QLED TVs:** 
QLED stands for “Quantum Light Emitting Diode”. This technology uses quantum dot technology, which is made up of a layer of tiny nanoparticles that emit different colors of light when stimulated by energy. QLED TVs come with an advanced layer of quantum dots located between the LED backlight and the LCD panel. This is responsible for producing brighter and more vivid colors than conventional LCD panels.

**OLED TVs:**
OLED stands for Organic Light Emitting Diode. These kinds of TVs produce their own light. This means that each pixel on the display panel has its own light, which enables OLED TVs to have a much wider viewing angle than most other types of TVs. The result is true black and infinite contrast ratios.

### Color Accuracy

QLED TVs rely on LED backlight, which affects the accuracy of the color black because LED light can bleed into darker areas. This might result in blacks looks somewhat gray, reducing the overall contrast. On the other hand, OLED TVs offer an infinite contrast ratio, as each pixel produces its own light. This means that OLED TVs offer truer blacks and darker overall visuals. OLED is the winner when it comes to color accuracy.

### HDR Performance

HDR (High Dynamic Range) is a technology that facilitates a better and more accurate representation of darker and brighter scenes in pictures. OLED TVs can produce brighter and more varied colors, and therefore often handle HDR better than QLED TVs. The OLED TVs are capable of a higher peak brightness, and a greater range of shadow detail. This results in a better gradient of color, and a much more precise representation of shadows. OLED is again the clear winner when it comes to HDR performance.

### Viewing Angle

QLED TVs can experience a certain degree of discoloration when viewed off-center. As mentioned earlier, QLED TVs rely on backlighting, and viewing them from an angle might diminish the brightness and color accuracy. With OLED TVs, this is not an issue, because each pixel produces its own light. This means that there is no color or brightness loss when viewing from different angles. The OLED TVs take the lead when it comes to viewing angles.

### Price

OLED TVs are more expensive than QLED TVs, the reason for this is the production costs and challenges in manufacturing large-size OLED panels. QLED TVs are more affordable  than OLED TVs and are within reach of most customers.

### Conclusion

When it comes to 4K viewing, both QLED and OLED technologies have their benefits. OLED TVs come with unbeatable color accuracy, HDR performance, and viewing angles, and while the QLED TVs are more affordable, they don't quite match up to what OLED TVs offer in terms of viewing experience.
