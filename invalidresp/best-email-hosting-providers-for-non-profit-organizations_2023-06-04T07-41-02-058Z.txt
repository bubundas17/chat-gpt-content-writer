If you're running a non-profit organization, you know how important it is to stay connected with your team, donors, and supporters. Having a reliable email hosting solution is crucial to ensure effective communication, collaboration, and fundraising. But with so many email hosting providers in the market, it can be overwhelming to choose the right one for your non-profit's needs and budget.

In this blog post, we'll review some of the best email hosting providers for non-profit organizations. We'll compare their pricing, features, and support to help you make an informed decision.

1. G Suite

G Suite is a cloud-based email hosting solution by Google that offers professional email addresses, 30GB of cloud storage, and other productivity tools such as Google Docs, Sheets, and Slides. G Suite's email hosting has enterprise-grade security features such as two-factor authentication, spam filters, and TLS encryption. It also integrates with other Google services like Google Calendar and Google Meet for seamless communication and scheduling. G Suite is ideal for non-profits that need a scalable and reliable email hosting solution with advanced collaboration features.

Pricing: G Suite Basic plan starts at $6/user/month, G Suite Business plan starts at $12/user/month.

2. Microsoft 365

Microsoft 365 (formerly Office 365) is another cloud-based email hosting solution that offers professional email addresses, 1TB of cloud storage, and other productivity tools such as Word, Excel, and PowerPoint. It also includes enterprise-level security features such as Advanced Threat Protection and Data Loss Prevention. Microsoft 365's email hosting integrates with other Microsoft services like OneDrive and Teams for enhanced collaboration. Non-profits that need an email hosting solution with advanced security features and Microsoft's productivity tools should consider Microsoft 365.

Pricing: Microsoft 365 Business Basic plan starts at $5/user/month, Microsoft 365 Business Standard plan starts at $12.50/user/month.

3. Zoho Mail

Zoho Mail is a web-based email hosting solution that offers custom domain email addresses, 50GB of cloud storage, and other collaboration features such as calendars and tasks. Zoho Mail also includes advanced security features such as SPF and DKIM authentication, antivirus and anti-spam filters, and TLS encryption. Zoho Mail's email hosting integrates with other Zoho services like CRM and Projects for efficient management and communication. Non-profits that need an affordable and feature-rich email hosting solution should consider Zoho Mail.

Pricing: Zoho Mail's Standard plan starts at $1/user/month, Zoho Mail's Professional plan starts at $4/user/month.

4. ProtonMail

ProtonMail is a web-based email hosting solution that focuses on privacy and security. It offers custom domain email addresses, 5GB of encrypted storage, and end-to-end encryption for all emails. ProtonMail's email hosting does not require any personal information to create an account and ensures anonymous and private communication. ProtonMail is ideal for non-profits that deal with sensitive information and need a highly secure email hosting solution.

Pricing: ProtonMail's Plus plan starts at $5/user/month, ProtonMail's Professional plan starts at $8/user/month.
